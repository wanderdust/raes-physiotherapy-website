Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about physiotherapy, our services, and what to expect.

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that helps people affected by injury, illness or disability through movement, exercise, manual therapy, education and advice. Our physiotherapists use evidence-based techniques to help restore function, reduce pain, and prevent future injury.

No, you don't need a GP referral to book an appointment at Rae's Anatomy Physiotherapy. You can self-refer directly by calling us on 07588 468380 or booking online. However, if you're using private health insurance, some providers may require a GP referral.

Initial assessments typically last 60 minutes, return visits are 45 minutes, and follow-up sessions are 30 minutes. This gives us enough time to assess your condition thoroughly, provide treatment, and give you exercises or advice to help between sessions.

The number of sessions varies depending on your condition, injury severity, and treatment goals. Many patients see improvement within 3–6 sessions, but some conditions may require more. Your physiotherapist will discuss a treatment plan with you during your initial assessment.

Yes, we work with most major private health insurance providers including Vitality, Aviva, AXA, Cigna, and Healix. We can provide direct billing for approved treatments. Please check with your provider for coverage details and any authorisation requirements.

Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. For lower body treatments, shorts are ideal. For upper body issues, a t-shirt or vest top works well. You may need to remove some clothing for assessment and treatment.

Yes, physiotherapy is highly effective for managing chronic pain conditions. We use a combination of manual therapy, exercise prescription, pain education, and self-management strategies to help reduce pain and improve function. Many patients experience significant improvements in their quality of life.

We require at least 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Cancellations with less than 24 hours notice or missed appointments may be subject to a cancellation fee. Please see our full booking and cancellation policy for details.

Yes, we offer home and community physiotherapy for patients who are unable to attend our clinics. This service is ideal for those who are housebound, have mobility issues, or have just been discharged from hospital. Contact us to discuss your needs.

We treat a wide range of conditions including sports injuries, back and neck pain, post-operative rehabilitation, chronic pain, arthritis, vestibular disorders, workplace injuries, and more. Our physiotherapists have extensive experience across many areas. If you're unsure whether we can help, please call us for a free 10-minute consultation.
