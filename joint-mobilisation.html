Joint Mobilisation

Improve joint mobility and reduce pain with expert physiotherapy

Professional Joint Mobilisation

Joint mobilisation is a skilled manual therapy technique used to restore movement and reduce pain in stiff or painful joints. Our physiotherapists apply carefully controlled forces to the joint to improve the quality and range of movement.

Joint mobilisation is often used alongside exercise therapy and other physiotherapy interventions to provide a comprehensive and effective treatment approach.

Joints We Treat:

  • Spinal joints (cervical, thoracic, lumbar)
  • Shoulder joint and shoulder girdle
  • Hip joint
  • Knee joint
  • Ankle and foot joints
  • Wrist and hand joints
  • Rib joints (costovertebral)

Benefits of Joint Mobilisation:

  • Reduces joint pain and stiffness
  • Improves range of movement
  • Enhances joint mechanics
  • Reduces muscle guarding
  • Promotes healing of joint tissues
  • Improves functional movement
  • Complements active exercise programmes

What to Expect

During your assessment, our physiotherapist will identify the specific joints that are restricted or painful. Mobilisation is then applied using gentle, rhythmic movements at varying grades depending on the level of restriction and pain.

Most patients find joint mobilisation comfortable and experience improvement in movement and pain levels during or immediately after treatment.
